About Us

Cultivating Vedic Values

By helping Cows

 In 2016, the Government of Rajasthan and Jaipur Nagar Nigam partnered with The AkshayaPatra Foundation, Jaipur, Rajasthan to manage the Hingonia Cattle Rehabilitation Centerwhich is the largest facility dedicated to the welfare and rehabilitation of stray cattle under the animal welfare program.

For this cause a separate trust was registered. The trust, a non-profit organization, is committed to the welfare of the stray, accidental and old cattles.

The trust consists of 3 government officials, 3 independent trustees, and 9 missionary trustees, all devoted to serving the Hingonia Cattle Rehabilitation Center. The operations are managed by full-time missionaries from The Akshaya Patra Foundation and Hare Krishna Movement Jaipur, who have embraced the humble service to mother cows.

Since the trust took charge in 2016, the number of cattle at the centre has grown from just over 6,000 to more than 20,000 in 2024. The Jaipur Municipal Corporation ambulance brings around 100 stray cows to the centre daily. These cows are accidental and diseased. Our dedicated team of 25 doctors and 30 compounders treat them with care.

Several initiatives have been undertaken to make the centre self-sustaining, such as solar on grid, the development of a biogas plant and the sale of dairy products to increase revenue generation.

Initially, the centre faced numerous challenges, including high cattle mortality rates due to rains and poor infrastructure. However, with the support of Jaipur Nagar Nigam and generous donors, the infrastructure has significantly improved, leading to better management systems and processes. Enhancements like a state-of-the-art ICU and medical care facilities have notably reduced cattle mortality rates.

Our mission is to ensure the well-being of the cattle at Hingonia through continuous improvement and sustainable practices. We are dedicated to providing a safe and nurturing environment for the stray cattle and our centre not only play a crucial role in ensuring the wellbeing of these abandoned animals but also contributes significantly to road safety by preventing accidents caused by stray cattles.

Our Vision
To ensure that every stray and accidental cows is well-fed and cared for.
Our Mission
To provide nourishment and care to 30,000 stray and accidental cows by 2030.
Whom We Serve
We undertake the compassionate mission of rescuing and rehabilitating stray and accidental cows in the Jaipur metropolitan region. These gentle creatures are offered a sanctuary filled with love, care, and respect.
Our Approach
When we rescue these cows, our first priority is their well-being. We provide them with immediate medical attention and ensure they are in good health. Once they are stable, we welcome them into our sanctuary, where they are embraced with compassion and kindness.

Hingonia: A Heaven for Cows

The Hingonia Cattle Rehabilitation Centre stands as a sanctuary for abandoned and injured cows. It is the largest facility of its kind in Jaipur, Rajasthan, equipped with a state-of-the-art hospital, two ICU units, and 30 operative wards where we care for these stray cattle. Here, the Jaipur Municipal Corporation brings all accidental and diseased cattle to receive the care they need. Hingonia is truly a heaven for cows, offering them a second chance at life. Through continuous improvement and sustainable practices, we are dedicated to providing a safe and nurturing environment for all the cattle under our care.

Hingonia: A Self-Suatainable Center

The Hingonia Cattle Rehabilitation Center, handed over to the Trust in 2016, initially housed around 6,000 cows. Today, it has grown to accommodate more than 20,000 cows. To meet the needs of our expanding cattle population, we recently constructed a shed for 3,000 cows and need to build additional shelters home for another 4,000 stray cows. To efficiently feed our large cattle population, we utilize six Total Mixed Ration (TMR) fodder feeding machines. However, as our cattle numbers continue to rise, we need three more TMR machines to ensure all stray cows receive adequate nutrition. Hingonia is a model of sustainability and innovation, providing a safe haven for thousands of stray cows. Our continuous efforts to enhance infrastructure, implement efficient feeding systems, and adopt renewable energy sources ensure that we can sustainably care for our ever-growing stray cattle population.
